My main research line examines how elastic waves propagate through **periodic metamaterial unit cells** in two dimensions. By changing a unit cell's geometry and material distribution, phononic band gaps - frequency ranges in which waves are strongly attenuated - can be opened, shifted, and widened.

The work uses the open-source finite element framework [Firedrake](https://www.firedrakeproject.org/) and the [PETSc](https://petsc.org/) solver stack to formulate periodic elastic eigenvalue problems and compute dispersion relations across the unit-cell Brillouin zone.
